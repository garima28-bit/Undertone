The method

Three steps. No trace.

  1. 01

    Choose a carrier

    Any lossless PNG, WAV, or FLAC works as a host — the file that will carry your secret without ever showing it.

  2. 02

    Embed the payload

    Your message is encrypted first, then its bits are distributed across the least‑significant channels of the carrier.

  3. 03

    Share it anywhere

    The result looks, sounds, and plays exactly like the original — because to every eye but the right key, it is.

Built for scrutiny

🔒

AES‑256 before embedding

Payloads are encrypted before a single bit touches the carrier — extraction without the key returns noise, nothing more.

📊

Statistically quiet

Embedding is calibrated to hold chi‑square and RS‑analysis distributions steady, so nothing flags the carrier as altered.

⌁

Runs entirely offline

Every encode and decode happens on‑device, in‑browser. Your carrier and your payload never leave your machine.

◧

Lossless carriers only

Caché refuses lossy formats like JPEG or MP3 by default — compression is the one thing that can shake a payload loose.

Under the hood

One bit per pixel is all it takes.

A 12‑megapixel photo has room for roughly 4.5 million bits in its lowest plane alone — enough for a page of encrypted text, a signed document, or a set of coordinates, folded into color values that shift by a single, imperceptible degree.
